# PTO and Leave Policy

## Overview

This document describes the Paid Time Off (PTO) policy at AcmeCorp. It applies
to all full-time and part-time regular employees in the United States. Contract
and temporary workers are governed by their respective contracts and are not
covered here.

## PTO accrual

Full-time employees accrue PTO on a pay-period basis. New hires accrue PTO
starting on their first day of employment and may begin using accrued PTO
after completing 30 days of continuous service.

| Tenure | Annual PTO (days) | Bi-weekly accrual |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 2 years | 15 | 0.577 |
| 2 – 5 years | 20 | 0.769 |
| 5 – 10 years | 25 | 0.962 |
| 10+ years | 30 | 1.154 |

The PTO policy for new hires is straightforward: you accrue from day one,
but you may only request paid time off after the 30-day waiting period.
Unpaid leave during the waiting period requires manager approval.

## Carryover

Up to 5 days of unused PTO may be carried over into the next calendar year.
Anything above 5 days is forfeited on January 1. We strongly encourage
managers to plan team coverage so that employees can use their accrued time.

## Sick leave

Sick leave is separate from PTO. Employees receive 10 paid sick days per year,
non-accruing, available from day one. Sick leave does not carry over.

## Bereavement and jury duty

Up to 5 days of bereavement leave is available for the loss of an immediate
family member. Jury duty is paid for the duration of service with valid
documentation.

## How to request PTO

1. Submit a request in WorkdayPTO at least 5 business days in advance for
   non-emergency leave.
2. For emergency leave (illness, family emergency), notify your manager as
   soon as practical.
3. Approval is at the discretion of the manager based on team coverage.

## FAQ

**Q: I'm a new hire. When do I start accruing PTO?**
A: From your first day of employment. You can use accrued PTO after the
30-day waiting period.

**Q: What happens to my PTO if I leave AcmeCorp?**
A: In states that require it (e.g., California), unused accrued PTO is paid
out at termination. In other states, refer to your offer letter.

**Q: Can I take PTO before I have accrued it?**
A: With manager approval, you may borrow up to 3 days of PTO against future
accrual. If you leave before earning it back, the negative balance is
deducted from your final paycheck.
